**Sunscreen filter used**

Diethylamino Hydroxybenzoyl Hexyl Benzoate (Univul A Plus) which has a very high UVA protection and very photostable and Ethylhexyl Triazone

  

**Star ingredients**

Niacinamide, Adenosine, Panthenol, Beta-Glucan, Sea Buckthorn Oil

  

  

**How to use this?**

Apply liberally 15 minutes before sun exposure. Reapply every 3 hours when exposed to sunlight.

It's between cream and gel texture. Not a thick as cream but not as light as gel texture. It feels slightly greasy upon applying but it absorbs quite fast with no white cast and no sticky feeling. It also goes well under my makeup and didn't make my skin feels tight and patchy.

  

  
  

  

**My thoughts**

When I saw Klairs is launching a new sunscreen, I was excited because I always love Klairs product and this sunscreen specifically mentioned about no whitecast and no alcohol. This is a chemical sunscreen which is great for people who hates the white cast and greasiness from physical sunscreen. Since I have dry skin, I would pretty much avoid sunscreen that has no alcohol. I still need to use a moisturizer underneath this sunscreen but I like how this sunscreen doesn't makes my skin feels tight or dry. 

  

It also didn't cause any irritation or redness to my skin. However, please do a patch test if your skin can't tolerate essential oils. As for me, I'm okay with them.

  

I am not sure whether this is a waterproof sunscreen but it's a great sunscreen for everyday use since it comes in 80ml which is quite big than most sunscreen in the market. Overall, I am so happy with this sunscreen!
